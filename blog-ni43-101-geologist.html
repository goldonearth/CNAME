NI 43-101 Reporting & How to Find the Right Geologist

Published: March 2026 • Reading Time: 14 minutes

Whether you're selling a Canadian mining asset or seeking investment, understanding NI 43-101 technical reports and working with qualified geologists is critical. This guide explains the standard and how to find the right expert for your transaction.

What is NI 43-101?

NI 43-101 (National Instrument 43-101) is Canada's standardized code for public disclosure of mineral project information. It ensures technical reports are accurate, complete, and prepared by qualified professionals.

Key requirements:

  • Must be prepared or supervised by a "Qualified Person" (QP)
  • Must follow strict guidelines for resource estimation, sampling, and data verification
  • Required for public companies listing on Canadian exchanges (TSX, TSXV)
  • Increasingly requested by international buyers for due diligence

Who is a "Qualified Person" (QP)?

A QP under NI 43-101 must have:

  • Relevant university degree in geoscience, engineering, or related field
  • Minimum 5 years of experience relevant to the subject matter
  • Experience in mineral exploration, mine development, or mineral production
  • Good standing with a recognized professional association (e.g., P.Geo., P.Eng.)

Important: A QP takes legal responsibility for the technical content of the report. This accountability is why their expertise is non-negotiable.

When Do You Need an NI 43-101 Report?

You typically need an NI 43-101 compliant report when:

  • Selling a Canadian mining asset to institutional buyers
  • Raising capital from public markets or sophisticated investors
  • Providing due diligence documentation in cross-border transactions
  • Supporting reserve/resource claims in marketing materials

How to Find the Right Geologist for Your Project

Not all geologists are created equal. Follow this framework to find the right expert:

Step 1: Define Your Needs

  • Project Stage: Exploration, resource definition, feasibility, or production?
  • Commodity Focus: Gold, copper, polymetallic? Different expertise required.
  • Report Type: Technical report, resource estimate, due diligence review?
  • Timeline & Budget: NI 43-101 reports typically cost $25k-$150k+ and take 2-6 months.

Step 2: Verify Credentials

  • Confirm professional designation (P.Geo., P.Eng.) with provincial association
  • Check NI 43-101 experience: Ask for examples of reports they've signed as QP
  • Verify commodity expertise: A porphyry copper expert may not be ideal for epithermal gold
  • Review conflict of interest disclosures: QP must be independent for public disclosures

Step 3: Evaluate Experience

  • Ask about projects in similar geological settings to yours
  • Request references from past clients (mine owners, companies)
  • Review their technical publications or conference presentations
  • Assess their understanding of current market expectations for resource reporting

Step 4: Assess Communication & Fit

  • Can they explain complex technical concepts clearly?
  • Are they responsive and professional in communications?
  • Do they understand your commercial objectives, not just technical requirements?
  • Will they work collaboratively with your legal and financial advisors?

Red Flags When Hiring a Geologist

  • Guarantees specific resource grades or tonnages before drilling
  • Unwilling to provide references or examples of past NI 43-101 work
  • Quotes significantly below market rates (may indicate inexperience or cutting corners)
  • Cannot clearly explain their QP status or professional standing
  • Pressures you to skip independent verification steps

Working with Geologists in Transactions

When selling your mine, the right geologist adds value by:

  • Preparing credible technical documentation that builds buyer confidence
  • Identifying value-enhancing opportunities (e.g., resource expansion potential)
  • Providing expert testimony or Q&A support during buyer due diligence
  • Helping structure earn-in or royalty agreements based on technical milestones

Conclusion

NI 43-101 compliance and qualified geologist expertise are critical for maximizing value in Canadian mining transactions. Investing in the right technical partner protects your interests and enhances credibility with serious buyers.
