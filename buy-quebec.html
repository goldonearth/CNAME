Why Quebec Gold Mines Attract Investors

Quebec has emerged as one of the world's most attractive mining jurisdictions, combining world-class geology in the Abitibi and James Bay regions with a government actively supporting mining development. For investors, Quebec offers significant exploration upside, clear regulatory framework, and excellent infrastructure.

Key Investment Advantages

  • World-Class Geology: Extension of Abitibi Greenstone Belt, James Bay emerging district
  • Government Support: Plan Nord initiative, mining tax incentives, infrastructure investment
  • Excellent Infrastructure: Hydroelectric power (lowest rates in North America), road networks
  • Clear Regulatory Framework: Transparent permitting process, defined timelines
  • Strong Buyer Interest: Agnico Eagle, Yamana, and other majors actively acquiring Quebec assets

Quebec-Specific Due Diligence

Technical Due Diligence Checklist

  1. NI 43-101 Compliance: Verify resource/reserve estimates are NI 43-101 compliant with Qualified Person sign-off
  2. Geological Review: Independent verification of drill data, geological model, resource estimation
  3. Mining Engineering: Mine plan review, production schedule validation, equipment assessment
  4. Metallurgical Testing: Recovery rate verification, processing method suitability
  5. Infrastructure Assessment: Road access, power supply (Hydro-Québec), water availability

Legal & Regulatory Due Diligence

  1. Mining Lease Status: Verify all claims are in good standing with MERN
  2. BAPE Review: Check if environmental review required, status of any reviews
  3. First Nations Consultation: Verify duty to consult fulfilled, IBAs in place
  4. Environmental Compliance: Review Certificates of Authorization, inspection reports
  5. Closure Plan: Review approved rehabilitation plan and financial guarantee

Valuation Methods for Quebec Mines

Abitibi & James Bay Valuation

Quebec's gold districts have unique characteristics that affect valuation:

  • Grade: Typically 3-10 g/t for underground operations - competitive with global deposits
  • Tonnage: Varies from small high-grade veins to large bulk-tonnage deposits
  • Metallurgy: Generally straightforward processing with high recovery rates
  • Infrastructure: Hydro-Québec power significantly reduces operating costs
Quebec Investment Benchmarks (2026):
• Producing mines: $800-$2,500 per proven ounce
• Advanced development: $250-$700 per indicated ounce
• Exploration properties: $80-$250 per inferred ounce
• James Bay region: 20-40% premium for emerging district potential

Western Quebec extension of Ontario's Abitibi Belt. Underexplored relative to Ontario side.

Investment Profile: Exploration upside, geological potential

Financing Your Quebec Mine Acquisition

Common Financing Structures

💵 All-Cash Purchase

Cleanest structure, fastest closing. Requires significant capital but avoids debt service.

🏦 Bank Financing

Traditional debt financing. Typically 50-70% LTV for producing mines.

🤝 Vendor Financing

Seller finances portion of purchase. Higher total price but preserves capital.

📊 TSX Financing

Public market financing through TSX/TSXV. Flow-through shares available for exploration.

Timeline for Buying a Quebec Gold Mine

Typical Acquisition Timeline

  1. Target Identification (1-2 months): Define investment criteria, identify targets
  2. Preliminary Due Diligence (1-2 months): Initial technical review, site visit
  3. First Nations Consultation (3-12 months): If IBA not in place, can extend timeline
  4. Letter of Intent (2-4 weeks): Negotiate key terms, sign LOI
  5. Detailed Due Diligence (2-4 months): Comprehensive technical, legal, environmental review
  6. Definitive Agreement (1-2 months): Negotiate and execute purchase agreement
  7. Closing (2-4 months): MERN approvals, First Nations approvals, funding

Total Timeline: 10-18 months for producing mines with IBA, 18-36 months if BAPE review or First Nations consultation required
